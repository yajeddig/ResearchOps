- **Insights**: Legacy architectures (Purdue pyramid, vendor lock-in) hinder innovation and profitability. Successful transformation requires architectural shifts (UNS, NOA, OPA), technological sovereignty (open standards like OPC UA, MQTT, Sparkplug B), and data industrialization (DataOps). The "project-by-project" approach leads to "pilot purgatory"; an infrastructure-first approach yields higher ROI. Human capital (fusion teams, internal expertise) is critical.
- **Actionable takeaways**:
    - Adopt event-driven architectures (Unified Namespace) and hybrid models (NAMUR Open Architecture) to liberate data securely.
    - Embrace open standards (OPC UA, MQTT, Sparkplug B) and initiatives like Open Process Automation (OPA) to reduce vendor lock-in.
    - Invest in a unified data infrastructure (DataOps) before scaling individual digital use cases.
    - Build "Fusion Teams" combining OT and IT expertise, and re-internalize core competencies like system architecture and cybersecurity.
    - Prioritize data readiness and cybersecurity (IEC 62443) as prerequisites for successful AI deployment.
