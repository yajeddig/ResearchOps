## 🎯 Relevance
This analysis is useful for understanding current investment trends impacting industrial sectors, identifying strategic challenges related to technical debt in heavy assets, and justifying the necessity of modernization investments for industrial systems to maintain competitive advantage and long-term value.

## 📖 Content
🏭 HALO Effect — Goldman Sachs (24/02/2026)

GS lance la thèse HALO : Heavy Assets, Low Obsolescence. En résumé : taux élevés + relocalisation + IA qui érode les marges du "Capital Light" (software/IT) → le marché re-valorise les actifs physiques lourds (réseaux, pipelines, utilities, infra transport). Leur panier Capital Intensive surperforme le Capital Light de 35% depuis 2025.

La nuance : GS confond "difficile à disrupter par une startup" avec "pas obsolescent". Quiconque bosse dans l'industrie sait que ces actifs lourds croulent sous la dette technique (SCADA vétustes, sous-investissement chronique, digitalisation en retard). L'obsolescence n'est pas externe, elle est déjà dedans.

Verdict : thèse valide en pricing boursier relatif, simpliste sur le plan opérationnel. Ces "forteresses" ont besoin d'investissements massifs en modernisation pour justifier leur re-valorisation.

## 💡 Key Insights
- Goldman Sachs' HALO Effect thesis proposes a re-valuation of 'Heavy Assets, Low Obsolescence' (industrial physical assets) due to high interest rates, reshoring, and AI eroding 'Capital Light' (software/IT) margins.
- Capital Intensive assets have reportedly outperformed Capital Light assets by 35% since 2025, supporting the HALO thesis in relative stock pricing.
- The thesis is critiqued for conflating 'hard to disrupt by a startup' with 'not obsolescent', highlighting that heavy industrial assets often suffer from significant internal technical debt (outdated SCADA, chronic underinvestment, delayed digitalization).
- To justify their re-valuation and maintain long-term value, these industrial 'fortresses' require massive investments in modernization, addressing their internal obsolescence.
