Key words: Augmentation, automation, Industry 4.0, Industry 5.0, manufacturing.
A framework spanning out automation versus augmentation on a horizontal axis and point solutions versus system solutions on a vertical axis, as shown in Figure 1, can help demystify Industry 4.0.
First, there is a foundational layer of enabling digital technologies (see bottom of Figure 2) that collect, transmit, store, and protect data (e.g., [26]). These technologies have no use case on their own but enable the potential of other technologies.
Point automation technologies remove humans from process steps.
Point augmentation technologies assist workers in doing their work better, faster, or cheaper.
System automation (i.e., true Industry 4.0) is more realistically realized when there is an orchestrated use of many point automation technologies.
System augmentation... hosts technologies that aim to improve cross-process tasks, such as control tower technologies, supply chain software, and more traditional business management software, such as enterprise resource planning (ERP) and manufacturing execution systems (MES).
However, in the early stages of a transformation, focusing considerable efforts on point augmentation is a better strategy. Why? Because using new technologies to help people succeed at their job tasks rather than making them obsolete is not only good for morale but also frees up human ingenuity for the next improvement.
Companies that pursue a strategically selected and well-balanced portfolio of use cases from all four quadrants may be best positioned to reap exponential business benefits.
